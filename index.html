My Mission

Based on my understanding, human’s visual perception can be divided into three stages. The first stage is detection and recognition of scene objects which are invariant to viewpoints and orientations. Second, human is able to perform scene understanding, like the inference of viewpoint, orientation, depth, and even geo-location from a single image. Finally, human is able to reconstruct 3D scene from a single 2D image based on geographic relations. However, those tasks are hard for today’s computer vision system as lack of model robustness.

I proposed several solutions for robust feature learning based on different types of self-supervision. I also explored graphic methods to learn the view-invariant representations to perform cross-view matching. In the future, I plan to work on robust 3D scene understanding and reconstruction on the basis of computer graphics, and validate them in novel real-world application scenarios.

Publications

MSNet: A Multilevel Instance Segmentation Network for Natural Disaster Damage Assessment in Aerial Videos
Zhu, X., Liang, J., Hauptmann, A.
WACV 2021
PDF  Code
We present the first natural disaster building damage assessment dataset. It provides the first quantitative benchmark for assessing damage assessment in aerial videos. We introduce MSNet, which contains Hierarchical Region Proposal Network and Score Refinement Network to tackle the challgense brought by huge view-point variances.
Weakly Supervised 3D Semantic Segmentation Based on Cross-Image Consensus and Inter-Voxel Affinity Relations
Zhu, X., Chen, J., Zeng, X., Liang, J., Li, C., Zhang, J., Liu, S., Ban, X., Xu, M.
AAAI 2021 (under review)
We introduce CIVA-Net, the first 3D volumetric segmentation model based on image-level supervision. It contains two novel designs. The first is an inner-image consensus learning module to tackle the challenges brought by imaging limits. The second is an inter-voxel affinity learning module to predict segmentation with accurate boundaries of complex 3D structures.
Unsupervised 3D Semantic Segmentation Based on Multi-Level Self-Supervision
Zhu, X., Zeng, X., Li, C., Liu, S., Ban, X., Xu, M.
Nature Machine Intelligence (under review)
We present an unsupervised segmentation method based on multi-level self-supervision. The feature maps produced by each level of self-supervision are ensembled at the end to produce robust and consistent semantic segmentation.
The Effects of Impulse Purchase Behavior on Post-Purchase Satisfaction in E-Marketplace: Relationship between Online Search Session and Consumer Review Sentiments
Zhu, X., Yu, Y., Wu, D., Zhang, H.
CSWIM 2018
We present the first quantitative model to analyze the relationship between impulse purchase and consumer review sentiment.
